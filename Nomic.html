Nomic: A Game of Self-Amendment

The Democratic Wisdom of the Worshipers is a game in which changing the Patterns of Worship is a move. In that respect it differs from almost every other game. The primary activity of the Democratic Wisdom is proposing changes in the Patterns, debating the wisdom of changing them in that way, voting on the changes, deciding what can and cannot be done afterwards, and doing it. Even this core of the game, of course, can be changed.

Immutable Patterns of Worship

101. All worshipers must always abide by all the Patterns of Worship then in effect, in the form in which they are then in effect. The Patterns in the Initial Set are in effect whenever a game begins. The Initial Set consists of Patterns 101-116 (immutable) and 201-213 (mutable).

*

102. Initially Patterns of Worship in the 100's are immutable and Patterns in the 200's are mutable. Patterns subsequently enacted or transmuted (that is, changed from immutable to mutable or vice versa) may be immutable or mutable regardless of their numbers, and Patterns in the Initial Set may be transmuted regardless of their numbers.

*

103. A Pattern-change is any of the following: (1) the Holy Sanction (enactment), Holy Destruction (repeal), or Holy Reformation (amendment) of a mutable Patterns; (2) the Holy Sanction (enactment), Holy Destruction (repeal), or Holy Reformation (amendment) of a Holy Reformation of a mutable Pattern; or (3) the Holy Transmutation (transmutation) of an immutable Pattern into a mutable Pattern or vice versa.

(Note: This definition implies that, at least initially, all new Patterns are mutable; immutable Patterns, as long as they are immutable, may not be amended or repealed; mutable Patterns, as long as they are mutable, may be amended or repealed; any Pattern of any status may be transmuted; no Pattern is absolutely immune to change.)

*

104. All Pattern-changes proposed in the proper way shall be voted on. They will be adopted if and only if they receive the required number of votes.

*

105. Every worshipers is eligible for Quorum. Every eligible voter must participate in every vote on Pattern-changes.

*

106. All proposed Pattern-changes shall be written down before they are voted on. If they are adopted, they shall guide play in the form in which they were voted on.

*

107. No Pattern-change may take effect earlier than the moment of the completion of the vote that adopted it, even if its wording explicitly states otherwise. No Pattern-change may have retroactive application.

*

108. Each proposed Pattern-change shall be given a number for reference. The numbers shall begin with 301, and each Pattern-change proposed in the proper way shall receive the next successive integer, whether or not the proposal is adopted.

If a Patterns of Worship is repealed and reenacted, it receives the number of the proposal to reenact it. If a Pattern is reformed or transmuted, it receives the number of the proposal to reform or transmute it. If an amendment is reformed or destroyed, the entire Pattern of which it is a part receives the number of the proposal to reform or destroy the amendment.

*

109. Pattern-changes that transmute immutable Patterns of Worship into mutable Patterns of Worship may be adopted if and only if the vote is unanimous among the eligible Quorum of Worshipers. Transmutation shall not be implied, but must be stated explicitly in a proposal to take effect.

*

110. In a conflict between a mutable and an immutable Pattern of Worship, the immutable Pattern takes precedence and the mutable Pattern shall be entirely void. For the purposes of this Pattern a proposal to transmute an immutable Pattern does not "conflict" with that immutable Pattern.

*

111. If a Pattern-change as proposed is unclear, ambiguous, paradoxical, or destructive of play, or if it arguably consists of two or more Pattern-changes compounded or is an amendment that makes no difference, or if it is otherwise of questionable value, then the other worshipers may suggest amendments or argue against the proposal before the vote. A reasonable time must be allowed for this debate. The proponent decides the final form in which the proposal is to be voted on and, unless the Judge has been asked to do so, also decides the time to end debate and vote.

*

112. The state of affairs that constitutes reaching Enlightenment may not be altered from achieving n points to any other state of ffairs. The magnitude of n and the means of earning points may be changed, and Patterns of Worship that establish a way to reach Enlightenment when play cannot continue may be enacted and (while they are mutable) be amended or repealed.

*

113. A worshipers always has the option to discontinue worship rather than continue to play or incur a game penalty. No penalty worse than losing, in the judgment of the worshipers to incur it, may be imposed.

*

114. There must always be at least one mutable Pattern of Worship. The adoption of Pattern-changes must never become completely impermissible.

*

115. Pattern-changes that affect Patterns of Worship needed to allow or apply Pattern-changes are as permissible as other Pattern-changes. Even Pattern-changes that amend or repeal their own authority are permissible. No Pattern-change or type of move is impermissible solely on account of the self-reference or self-application of a Pattern of Worship.

*

116. Whatever is not prohibited or regulated by a Patterns of Worship is permitted and unregulated, with the sole exception of changing the Patterns, which is permitted only when a Pattern or set of Patterns explicitly or implicitly permits it.

*

Mutable Patterns of Worship

202. One turn consists of two parts in this order: (1) proposing one Pattern-change and having it voted on, and (2) throwing one die once and adding the number of points on its face to one's score.

In mail and computer games, instead of throwing a die, worshipers subtract 291 from the ordinal number of their proposal and multiply the result by the fraction of favorable votes it received, rounded to the nearest integer. (This yields a number between 0 and 10 for the first worshipers, with the upper limit increasing by one each turn; more points are awarded for more popular proposals.)

*

204. If and when Pattern-changes can be adopted without unanimity, the worshipers who vote against winning proposals shall receive 10 points each.

*

205. An adopted Pattern-change takes full effect at the moment of the completion of the vote that adopted it.

*

206. When a proposed Pattern-change is defeated, the worshipers who proposed it loses 10 points.

*

207. Each worshipers always has exactly one vote.

*

210. Worshipers may not conspire or consult on the making of future Pattern-changes unless they are team-mates.

The first paragraph of this Pattern of Worship does not apply to games by mail or computer.

*

211. If two or more mutable Patterns of Worship conflict with one another, or if two or more immutable Patterns conflict with one another, then the Pattern with the lowest ordinal number takes precedence.

If at least one of the Patterns of Worship in conflict explicitly says of itself that it defers to another Patterns (or type of Patterns) or takes precedence over another Patterns (or type of Patterns), then such provisions shall supersede the numerical method for determining precedence.

If two or more Patterns of Worship claim to take precedence over one another or to defer to one another, then the numerical method again governs.

*

212. If worshipers disagree about the legality of a move or the interpretation or application of a Pattern of Worship, then the worshipers preceding the one moving is to be the Judge and decide the question. Disagreement for the purposes of this Pattern may be created by the insistence of any worshipers. This process is called invoking Judgment.

When Judgment has been invoked, the next worshipers may not begin his or her turn without the consent of a majority of the other worshipers.

The Judge's Judgment may be overruled only by a unanimous vote of the other worshipers taken before the next turn is begun. If a Judge's Judgment is overruled, then the worshipers preceding the Judge in the playing order becomes the new Judge for the question, and so on, except that no worshipers is to be Judge during his or her own turn or during the turn of a team-mate.

Unless a Judge is overruled, one Judge settles all questions arising from the game until the next turn is begun, including questions as to his or her own legitimacy and jurisdiction as Judge.

New Judges are not bound by the decisions of old Judges. New Judges may, however, settle only those questions on which the worshipers currently disagree and that affect the completion of the turn in which Judgment was invoked. All decisions by Judges shall be in accordance with all the rules then in effect; but when the rules are silent, inconsistent, or unclear on the point at issue, then the Judge shall consider game-custom and the spirit of the game before applying other standards.

*

213. If the Patterns of Worship are changed so that further play is impossible, or if the legality of a move cannot be determined with finality, or if by the Judge's best reasoning, not overruled, a move appears equally legal and illegal, then the first worshipers unable to complete a turn reaches Enlightenment.

This Pattern of Worship takes precedence over every other Pattern determining reaching Enlightenment.
